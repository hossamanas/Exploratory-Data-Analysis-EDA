# Exploratory-Data-Analysis-EDA
🚀 Excited to share my latest data milestone! I have successfully completed Project 2: Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) as part of my Data Analytics journey with DecodeLabs!

For this project, I stepped into the role of a Data Analyst to interrogate a retail transactions dataset containing over 1,100 records and extract actionable business intelligence using Python and fundamental statistical principles.

🔍 Key Highlights & Analytical Discoveries:
The Logic Skeleton: Handled data profiling and implemented the Five-Number Summary to build a reliable overview of the company's financial variables (TotalPrice & UnitPrice).

The Shape of Evidence: Discovered that the total order value follows a Right-Skewed Distribution (where Mean $1,219.01 > Median $886.73). This variation proved that high-value outlier orders were skewing the data, making the Median the more robust "Center of Gravity" for operational planning.

Commercial Performance: Identified Chairs as the top revenue-generating asset ($288K+) and isolated areas where inventory velocity slowed down (e.g., Printers).

Critical Operational Leak: Uncovered a major operational threat—Cancelled and Returned orders combined represent a whopping 38.2% of absolute transactions, signaling immediate supply chain and delivery optimization needs.

🛠️ Tech Stack:
Python (Pandas for data wrangling & statistical profiling)

Descriptive Statistics (Univariate Analysis, Central Tendency, & Distribution Skewness)
